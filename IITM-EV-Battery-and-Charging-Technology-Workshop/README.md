## Overview

The two-day workshop focused on electric vehicle battery technologies, charging infrastructure, power electronics, wireless charging, and vehicle-to-grid (V2G) systems.

The program brought together students, researchers, faculty members, and industry professionals to discuss emerging trends and challenges in sustainable transportation and industrial electronics.

This event marked the **first officially conducted IEEE IES HUBS & NODES Initiative activity in the Chennai region**.

---

## IEEE IES Support

The workshop was supported under the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society (IES) HUBS & NODES Initiative with partial financial assistance from IEEE IES.

The initiative enabled:
- Broader technical participation
- Industry–academia interaction
- Knowledge dissemination in EV technologies
- Professional networking opportunities

# Technical Sessions

## Session 1 – EV Battery Technology
**Speaker:** Dr. Gautham Ram Chandra Mouli

Topics covered:
- Li-ion and next-generation battery technologies
- Battery pack design
- Thermal management systems
- Battery Management Systems (BMS)
- Charging and ageing mechanisms
- Battery recycling and sustainability

---

## Session 2 – Multiport Chargers for Electric 2W/3W Vehicles
**Speaker:** Dr. Vishnu Mahadeva Iyer

Topics covered:
- Multiport charger architectures
- Power factor correction
- Harmonic mitigation
- Independent battery charging control
- High-efficiency charging systems

---

## Session 3 – Reconfigurable Power Electronics for EV Charging Hubs

Topics covered:
- Three-phase PFC architectures
- Soft-switching techniques
- Flexible charging hub systems
- Multi-output charging systems

---

## Session 4 – Charging Technology for Heavy-Duty Vehicles
**Speaker:** Dr. Gautham Ram Chandra Mouli

Topics covered:
- Megawatt charging systems
- Charging infrastructure for buses and trucks
- Battery swapping technologies
- Electric heavy-duty transportation systems

---

## Session 5 – Wireless Charging Technology for EVs
**Speaker:** Dr. Deepak Ronanki

Topics covered:
- Static and dynamic wireless charging
- Coil and converter design
- EMC and efficiency challenges
- Modular charging systems

---

## Session 6 – Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) Technology
**Speaker:** Dr. Gautham Ram Chandra Mouli

Topics covered:
- Bidirectional charging
- Grid integration
- Energy management
- Battery degradation considerations
- International V2G case studies
